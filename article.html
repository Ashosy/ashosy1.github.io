                                                                                I have been a passive Quora user for quite a while, reading other peoples' theories and experiences.

                                                                                It was definitely a fun and rewarding experience without having to put myself on the spotlight.

                                                                                However, for a person who reads a lot, writing becomes an urging passion forcing its reader to

                                                                                share their view of the world. Hence, on the summer vacation of my freshman year of highschool,

                                                                                I wrote my first ever answer on Quora. The feedbacks, replies and compliments I got only pushed

                                                                                me to write further and on a consistent basis.

                                                      How I Ended Up In College In Ethiopia?

                                                                                I always had big dreams and Ethiopia was not one of them. At school, I was that kid that scored

                                                                                the highest and worked the least. I did not work hard, I just figured out techniques to crack school

                                                                                exams. I knew how to study and that's what got me past highschool and into college. However,

                                                                                getting into the right college mattered to me, hence I disciplined myself into taking the SAT's and

                                                                                going through the entire college application process. In a sudden turn of events, I ended up here in

                                                                                Ethiopia. Taking a teenager from the luxuries of life right into it's penury doesn't usually end up

                                                                                good, especially when they don't fit in. "Diaspora","Arab Ager" are things people label us with. I

                                                                                disliked every moment of my stay here. I lived alone in the country during my first year, which

                                                                                only made things worse. Not to mention, I was almost failing my studies. The techniques I used

                                                                                back in high school don't work in college. What I learnt is that college is more than some grades

                                                                                and a degree. How I see it, college mainly teaches you discipline, not hard work and not smart

                                                                                work, but discipline. I am still trying to master the art of disciplining myself, but I believe I still

                                                                                have a long way to go.


                                                      What I Think Of College?

                                                                               two main reasons. Number 1 : It's a transition stage from teenage life into adult life, from school

                                                                               into work and from dependency into independency. Number 2: It's like a bed to fall back on. In

                                                                               college, students take more risks exploring ideas and starting up a project since even if their projects

                                                                               didn't work they still have that degree to look forward to. After college, everything gets serious, there

                                                                               are bills to pay, food to buy etc. It's easier to stick to a riskless job which atleast pays the bill.


                                                      How I Started Reading?

                                                                              Reading was an indisputable activity in our family. Right after we learnt how to read letters, we

                                                                              were forced to read books even if we don't understand the content. My mom believed that real

                                                                              education does not come from schools but from the minds and to nurture our minds we had to read.

                                                                              She always said "The world has too many ignorant people with degrees, you will not be

                                                                              added to that pool." Every summer vacation meant a bunch of books to read. It was a hectic activity

                                                                              which neither me nor my siblings particularly enjoyed doing. As we grew up, our mother was rarely

                                                                              present with us. At first, it was such a relief from all the readings that we were supposed to do. But

                                                                              as time passed, we found joy in reading that we willingly started reading books more than we ever did.

                                                      What Volunteering Means To Me?

                                                                             I lived in a country where you get paid for everything you do even more than what you deserve,

                                                                             which people usually call it generosity. Volunteering was existent, but volunteering with no payment

                                                                             was downright madness. Hence, when I came to Ethiopia, I was pretty shocked when I saw people

                                                                             paying to volunteer. Volunteering as I saw it before was helping tourists get adjusted to the country,

                                                                             providing snacks and giving directions to visitors of the Holy Mosques. Never did I think that by

                                                                             volunteering I would actually help somebody's life become better even if just for a day. It gave such a

                                                                             weird sense of inner satisfaction: helping people without expecting anything in return. People usually

                                                                             tell me that they volunteer to give back to the community they lived in and then raise the question of

                                                                             why I volunteer. I volunteer not with the sense of gratitude and the urge of giving back but with the

                                                                             vision of making what is around me better. Although my class schedules usually clash with the time

                                                                             I am supposed to volunteer, I try as much as I can to fit everything into my schedule.

                                                                             That is the question people ask when I tell them I chose this field. To be honest, I sometimes ask

                                                                             myself the same question. Everyone including myself thought that I will join a law field or some

                                                                             health field. I was good with dealing with computers but only the basic stuff. I remember when my

                                                                             mom got us a computer tutor who could teach us some coding. That was a horror to me. On top of

                                                                             him being Indian, he did not understand a single word of Arabic and he taught us as if we were some

                                                                             coding experts. However bad of a coder I know myself to be, I joined this field with a goal and I am

                                                                             not getting out of it until I achieve that goal, however long it takes. I don't actually understand how

                                                                             people enjoy sitting for hours coding, but I don't enjoy it one bit. The only thing I like about it is that

                                                                             things are possible in the world of technology which otherwise are not. For me, coding is more of a

                                                                             tool I want to use to create what I imagine.
